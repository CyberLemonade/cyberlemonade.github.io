Boston

When I'm not at work you will most likely find me walking. Sometimes not even to someplace. I just enjoy walking. And Boston's very walkable too. If you're looking for good walking routes in Boston, try the Charles River Esplanade (great for when you need to cross the river), the Emerald Necklace (ends at the Arnold Arboretum, which one should see of its own accord), the Commonwealth Avenue Mall (for when you need to go downtown), or Beacon Street (I live on this street and as far as ordinary residential streets go it's top 5).

Some of my other favourite spots in Boston are The Brattle Theatre (movie projection from behind the screen!), The Coolidge Corner Theater (oldest theatre in Boston), any BBQ Chicken outlet (except maybe the Allston one, I'm not a fan of Allston), Mount Auburn Cemetery (Boston's response to Père Lachaise), the Boston MFA (they've got an original Dutch dollhouse from the 17th century, only three of these on display in the world right now), and the Harborwalk (this one just because I think it's really cool to walk along the Atlantic coast). Any place I missed you'll find in a top 10 list for Boston.

Books

When I'm tired, I'm probably at home reading a book. There's not really any overarching theme to the books I read, although if you squint hard enough, you might see a slight bias towards fantasy and historical fiction.

  • A recent novel that I really like is Babel.
  • A slightly older novel that I really like is After Dark.
  • A much older novel that I really like is The Master and Margarita.
  • A much, much older novel that I really like is Anna Karenina.
  • An ancient novel that I really like is Mahabharata.

If you like owls, you can check out the Guardians of Ga'Hoole books. If you like Victorian era thrillers, you can check out the Sally Lockhart books. Any other default recommendation I might come up with has already been suggested to you by a long line of individuals before me.

I'm also looking to collect books from bookstores that have their own stamps. For example, Shakespeare and Company and the Strand Bookstore do this. If you know of other such bookstores, please let me know.

Movies

When I'm not tired and it's the weekend, I'm almost definitely watching a movie. Similar to my bookshelf, my movie drawer has no coherent theme barring a mildly disproportionate number of animated flicks.

  • Check out the oldest feature-length animated movie ever made — The Adventures of Prince Achmed (1926).
  • Check out the most iconic animated movie ever made — Akira.
  • Check out the most consistent movie franchise ever made — Mission Impossible.
  • Check out a movie made in my native language — Goopy Gyne Bagha Byne (গুপী গাইন বাঘা বাইন).
  • Check out a random artsy trilogy made by a Polish dude — Three Colours.

I can come up with other recommendations but I would want to tailor them to the audience's interests. And for the time being, my Letterboxd will not be featured on my webpage.

I also love learning about visual effects in cinema; you can check out Corridor Crew if you're interested in this as well.
