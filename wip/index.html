About

I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Computer Science, Boston University, in the Theoretical Computer Science (TCS) research group, where I am advised by Prof. Sofya Raskhodnikova. I completed my undergraduate degree in mathematics and computer science at Chennai Mathematical Institute.

My research interests are in computational complexity and property testing. In property testing, I mostly work on query and time complexity lower bounds, though these insights sometimes lead to new upper bounds as well. In complexity theory, I'm working on formalising relativisation to better understand it both as a proof barrier and a proof technique.

Properties tested.

Halfspaces, unate functions, k-submodular functions, parenthesis languages, connected graphs, k-connected graphs.
