About

I am a Ph.D. student in the Theoretical Computer Science (TCS) research group at the Department of Computer Science, Boston University, where I am advised by Prof. Sofya Raskhodnikova. I completed my undergraduate degree in mathematics and computer science at Chennai Mathematical Institute.

My research interests are in computational complexity and property testing. In property testing, I mostly work on query and time complexity lower bounds, although sometimes these insights lead to new upper bounds as well. In complexity theory, I'm working on formalising relativisation to better understand it both as a proof barrier and a proof technique.
